Case Overview

A landmark decision that transformed American criminal justice by establishing the constitutional right to counsel for indigent defendants.

The Decision

The Court ruled that the Sixth Amendment right to counsel applies to state courts through the Fourteenth Amendment, requiring states to provide attorneys for indigent defendants in felony cases.

The Impact

Overturned Betts v. Brady (1942) and established that counsel is fundamental to fair trials, fundamentally changing criminal procedure across all states.

The Legacy

Created the foundation for the modern public defender system and ensured that poverty does not determine access to justice in criminal cases.

Case Timeline
